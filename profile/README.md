Rhizae Studio is a civic design, data storytelling, and public-interest technology studio.
It explores how accessible interfaces, research tools, and visual systems can make complex public issues easier to understand and act on.

## What We Build
- Civic and editorial websites
- Accessible design systems
- Data visualization prototypes
- Policy and research dashboards
- Public-interest software experiments
- Documentation for ethical, legible, and inclusive digital systems

## Core Themes
- Accessibility-first interface design
- Data journalism and visual explanation
- Community economic resilience
- Public policy communication
- Research transparency
- Civic engagement infrastructure

## Why This Exists
Public information is often technically available but practically inaccessible.

Rhizae Studio focuses on the gap between information existing and information being usable. The work here treats design, data, and software as tools for making research, policy, and civic systems more understandable to the people affected by them.

## Working Principles
- Make complex systems understandable
- Treat accessibility as a baseline requirement
- Design for trust, not just attention
- Document assumptions and methods
- Build tools that can be inspected, reused, and improved
- Prefer clarity over spectacle
## Status
This organization is a working lab. Some repositories are stable projects; others are prototypes, drafts, or research experiments.
